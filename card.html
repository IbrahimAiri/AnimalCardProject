Arabian Camel (Dromedary)

The dromedary has not occurred naturally in the wild for nearly 2,000 years

  • Scientific Name: Camelus dromedarius
  • Average Length: 1.8-2 meters
  • Average Lifespan: 40-50 years
  • Habitat: The wild

Is a large, even-toed ungulate with one hump on its back. It is the tallest of the three species of camel. The dromedary inhabited arid regions, including the Sahara Desert. The domesticated dromedary is generally found in the semi-arid to arid regions, it is also commonly used for riding and as a beast of burden.
